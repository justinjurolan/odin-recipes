Ginataang Monggo with Tofu and Malunggay

When it comes to Filipino food, there are not many limits as to the kind of ingredients you can add. After all, our cooks boast a great deal of knowledge in cooking with vegetables and various spices. But a component we can almost always go back to, and cook quite perfectly, is monggo or mung beans. This legume contains a great amount of protein, and it’s truly delicious when you incorporate the right ingredients. I can tell you now that it goes amazingly with a smooth, coconut flavor— hence, the delicious Ginataang Monggo with Tofu and Malunggay!

Ginataang Monggo with Tofu and Malunggay

Course: Main Course
Cuisine: Filipino recipe
Keyword: ginisang munggo, monggo, monggo recipe, stir fried tofu
Prep Time: 5 minutes
Cook Time: 1 hour
Total time: 1 hour and 5 minutes
Servings: 6 people
Calories: 387kcal
Author: Vanjo Merano

Ingredients

  • 8 ounces mung beans
  • 1 Knorr Pork Cube
  • 40 grams Knorr Ginataang Gulay Mix
  • 8 ounces fried tofu diced
  • 1 cup malunggay leaves
  • 1 onion chopped
  • 1 to mato diced (optional)
  • 6 cups water
  • 3 Tablespoons cooking oil

Instructions

  1. Saute garlic, onion, and tomato.
  2. Add mung beans. Saute for 2 minutes.
  3. Pour water in the pot. Let boil.
  4. Add Knorr Pork Cube. Let boil. Continue cooking until the mung beans are mushy. Note: add more water as needed.
  5. Dilute Knorr Ginataang Gulay Mix in 1 ½ cups water. Mix well. Pour into the pot.
  6. Add fried tofu. Cook for 2 minutes..
  7. Put the malunggay leaves in the pot and then season with ground black pepper and salt.
  8. Transfer to a serving plate. Serve. Share and enjoy!
